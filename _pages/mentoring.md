Mentoring is one of the most rewarding parts of my work. Whether it's guiding undergraduates through their first research publication, supporting a faculty colleague's teaching development, or showing up for a young person in the community, I believe in investing deeply in the people around me.

## EASY Lab Mentoring

The EASY Lab isn't just a research group---it's a mentoring pipeline. Students typically enter through the Genetic Algorithm Research Project (GARP) in CSSE220, where they get a taste of evolutionary computation. From there, promising students join the lab for independent study, senior projects, or multi-quarter research sequences.

Key elements of the lab's mentoring approach:
- **Moodle onboarding site** with structured introduction to lab tools and expectations
- **Weekly Fun Informal Research Meetings (FIRMs)** since Spring 2020
- **Spring Research Opportunity Presentations** (2021--2024) where students present to potential recruits
- Research journal accountability system, reflective essay assessments, and learning contracts
