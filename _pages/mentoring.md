Mentoring is one of the most rewarding parts of my work. Whether it's guiding undergraduates through their first research publication, supporting a faculty colleague's teaching development, or showing up for a young person in the community, I believe in investing deeply in the people around me.

## EASY Lab Mentoring

The EASY Lab isn't just a research group---it's a mentoring pipeline. Students typically enter through the Genetic Algorithm Research Project (GARP) in CSSE220, where they get a taste of evolutionary computation. From there, promising students join the lab for independent study, senior projects, or multi-quarter research sequences.

Key elements of the lab's mentoring approach:
- **Moodle onboarding site** with structured introduction to lab tools and expectations
- **Weekly Fun Informal Research Meetings (FIRMs)** since Spring 2020
- **Spring Research Opportunity Presentations** (2021--2024) where students present to potential recruits
- Research journal accountability system, reflective essay assessments, and learning contracts
- 13+ unique student co-authors on publications
- 35 letters of recommendation written

## Undergraduate Research at Indiana University

Before Rose-Hulman, I mentored undergraduate researchers at IU for 6 semesters through the Undergraduate Research Opportunities in Computing Program, working with 9 students. At least 2 mentees went on to graduate school.

## Big Brothers Big Sisters

From 2013 to 2019, I served as a "Big" through Big Brothers Big Sisters in Bloomington, Indiana. My mentee Trevor went through an incredibly difficult period---both of his parents were arrested---and I supported him through that crisis, eventually helping him apply to and attend Vincennes University.

I was honored to receive the **Indiana University Male Big of the Year Award** in 2019.

## Academic Advising

I advise 14--22 students per year. I developed a comprehensive advising system that includes 4-year academic plans, summer and post-graduation tracking, and personal interest notes. This system has been adopted by 3+ new faculty members in the department.
