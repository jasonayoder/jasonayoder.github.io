## Personal Identity & Philosophy
- Lifelong passion for understanding the human mind, beginning as a religious pursuit, evolving into philosophy, then AI and cognitive science
- Views himself and others as "embodied, embedded agents performing adaptive behaviors"
- Views intelligence as best understood through adaptive behavior rather than loaded terms like "intelligence" or "cognition"
- Influenced by Professor Douglas Hofstadter's concept of communicating "the essence" of an idea and his theory of analogical thinking
- Describes going through phases: perfectionist as a child, then "maximum grade for minimum effort" optimizer, then shift to intrinsic motivation during graduate school
- Teaching philosophy: "Teaching is the highest form of understanding" (Aristotle)
